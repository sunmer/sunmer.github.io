I'm Nima Boustanian, and here's my philosophy

Software products shouldn't be all about the details.

In this day and age, 95% of what we create is obsolete in 10 years. At least architects ages ago had the chance to build cultural monuments such as the Eiffel Tower, The Taj Mahal and The Pyramids. All still worth spending fortunes each year to protect and preserve. But even the most elegant of our software products today will be replaced with better, faster, and most importantly, newer, counterparts soon.

Hence me not getting stuck on details. I lead teams. I help organizations make informed choices. But most importantly - I get the big picture. I suggest we focus on creating value now, since all of our frustrations about that final pixel, defect or conversion metric won't dictate your success in the long run.
